Handling Missing Data

What is it?

Handling missing data involves strategies to manage data that is incomplete or missing in the dataset. This can include methods like imputation, where missing values are filled in based on statistical measures or machine learning algorithms.

Why do we use it?

We use these techniques to ensure the dataset is complete and to avoid errors in analysis. Missing data can lead to biased results and reduce the statistical power of the analysis if not properly handled.

How does it affect our dataset?

By handling missing data, we ensure that our dataset remains usable and accurate for model training and analysis. Proper handling can improve model performance and lead to more reliable predictions.
